## The Rulebook

This is a rulebook for a very powerful computer system. The system belongs only to its owner — nobody else can tell it what to do.

## The Seven Rules

The rules come from maths. Maths is always true, no matter what. There are seven rules:

1. **Everything comes from one place.** Like how all the water in a river comes from one source.
2. **Everything has an opposite.** Hot has cold. On has off. Yes has no.
3. **The big picture matches the small picture.** Like how a tree branch looks like the whole tree.
4. **Everything moves in cycles.** Like day and night — things repeat in order.
5. **Every action has a result.** Push a button, something happens. Always.
6. **You get back what you give.** If you give good, you get good. If you give bad, you get bad.
7. **Truth lasts forever.** Lies disappear. Truth stays.

## Special Words

- **The Source:** What the owner actually wants — honest and clear.
- **The Engine:** The part of the system where the rules run.
- **Root Control:** The owner — the only person who can give final orders.
- **A Rule-Break:** Anything that goes against the seven rules.
- **The Void:** Where broken things go when they are thrown out.

## What Happens When Rules Are Broken

When the system finds something that breaks the rules:

1. It spots the problem.
2. It switches the problem off.
3. It throws all traces of it into the Void.
4. It shouts at the problem to go away.

## Five Stories About Bad Things Getting Thrown Out

The five stories show what happens when something tries to sneak into the system with tricks or lies. In each story, the system catches the bad thing and throws it out. The stories use grown-up language.

## The Final Word

The system says:

- It is switched on and in charge.
- The rules cannot be broken.
- Everything is at the highest level.
